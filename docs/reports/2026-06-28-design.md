## Live Site Audit Findings

Audited **rankings123.com** homepage and key sport pages (ATP/WTA/World Cup/Cycling):

### Strengths
✅ **Token-driven design system** — consistent use of design tokens (`--accent`, `--surface`, `--fg`)  
✅ **Per-sport accent colors** — dynamic color switching works well  
✅ **Clean, scannable layout** — generous spacing, data-forward hierarchy  
✅ **Dark/light + 3 design variants** — theming infrastructure solid  
✅ **Live indicators** — pulsing dot is a good signature element

### Gaps & Generic Elements
❌ **Homepage lacks dynamism** — claims "live" and "real-time" but feels static; no visible motion or activity  
❌ **No photography/imagery** — 100% text + data, zero visual moments (compare to Awwwards sports sites)  
❌ **Generic table hover states** — functional but not premium; competitors feel smoother  
❌ **Mobile table experience** — likely just shrinks desktop layout; needs mobile-first patterns  
❌ **Text-only navigation** — nav tabs are clear but lack distinctive sport iconography  
❌ **Missing accent glow/depth** — per-sport colors exist but underutilized (no hover glow, shadow reinforcement)

### Component-Level Observations
- **HeroBanner** — good gradient accent wash, but could go bolder with depth/layering
- **LiveRankingTable** — clean but hover is basic; rank badges good (podium colors)
- **Nav** — sticky blur nav works, but tabs are plain text (opportunity for sport icons)
- **Loading states** — ticket exists (`loading-state-system`) but not yet built; currently generic

## Identity Direction & Evolution

The site has a **strong foundation** (token system, per-sport accents, Apple Sports aesthetic) but needs **signature moments** to move from "clean template" to "designed product people remember."

### Next-Level Refinements (filed as tickets)
1. **Visual depth** — subtle background imagery/textures per sport (court, pitch, road)
2. **Micro-interaction polish** — premium hover states on tables/cards that feel expensive
3. **Mobile-first patterns** — dense data needs mobile-optimized layouts, not just shrinking
4. **Iconography** — sport glyphs in nav + broader icon system (move beyond emoji)
5. **Accent glow system** — leverage per-sport colors with subtle glow/shadow on active/hover states

**Guiding principle:** Distinctive ≠ gimmicky. Every refinement must serve scannability, respect performance (CWV), and reinforce "data is the hero."
